Frequently Asked Questions

This page is intended to provide you with detailed information about the IRN, including details on how to set up devices, how to access the system, nets, registration and more. If you need further information, please contact us, and we will reply promptly.

Who are the IRN's partners and friends?

  • Freestar Network - For our licensed members, our multimode system is permanently connected to our partners, the Freestar Network. The IRN has various analogue and digital connections, and by having this permanent link with Freestar, many more links and connections are made available for the use of our members on so many different modes. There are various gateways, nodes and repeaters connected all around the world, which makes the partnership hugely exciting. You can learn more about the Freestar Network by visiting their website, https://freestar.network.

  • Zello Mode Radio (ZMR) - The IRN also enjoys the partnership with the Zello Mode Radio group, also known as ZMR. They have a Zello channel connected to our multimode system open to licensed operators, and another connected to the ‘non-RF’ channels, which are open to both licensed and non-licensed members. You can learn more about the ZMR group by visiting their website, https://www.zmr.us.

  • The ROAR Channel - The IRN has several other partners and friends, and some of them have their own channels hosted on the IRN Teamspeak server. One of them is the ROAR group (Republic Of Avalon Radio). They have their own Zello channel and for some nets, they are connected to our channels, again for licensed and non-licensed members. You can learn more about the ROAR group by visiting their website, https://vo1rv.com/network-radio.
